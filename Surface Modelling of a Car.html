Surface Modelling of a Car

Digital Reconstruction and Prototyping of a Ferrari 308 via Advanced Surface Modeling and 3D Printing

Project Summary:

Objective: To demonstrate proficiency in Class-A surfacing and digital fabrication by creating a 1:10 model, dimensionally accurate, manufacturable 3D model of a classic Ferrari 308, culminating in a physical scale prototype produced via FDM 3D printing.

Core Challenge: To translate 2D reference blueprints into a complex, watertight 3D model with high aesthetic fidelity, while ensuring the design was optimized for additive manufacturing processes.

Outcome: A successful end-to-end workflow from digital sculpting to tangible prototype, resulting in a detailed, 1:10 assembled scale model that validates the surfacing techniques and print preparation strategies used.


A high-quality screenshot of the completed Ferrari 308 assembly in Fusion 360.

Methodology & Technical Execution:

1. Advanced Surface Modeling in Fusion 360:
• Blueprint Referencing: : Orthographic images (front, rear, side, top) were imported as Canvas objects on their respective planes and calibrated for scale to serve as precise tracing guides.
• Surface Creation: The complex curvature of the car body (hood, fenders, roofline) was constructed by creating a network of T-Spline surfaces. Control points and edges were meticulously manipulated to match the blueprints and achieve smooth, continuous curvature (G1/G2 continuity).
• Solid Model Generation: The quilt of fitted surfaces was stitched into a closed, watertight body and converted to a solid component for downstream use. Separate components like wheels were created using Revolve and Sweep tools.


A screenshot of the model prepared in the slicer, highlighting layer lines and support structures.

2. Design for Additive Manufacturing (DFAM) & Slicing:
The finalized CAD model was prepared for 3D printing using Ultimaker Cura slicer software. Printer & Material:Prusa i3 MK3 printer with ABS filament selected for its good strength-to-weight ratio and surface finish.
Viscous Damper Integration:To provide the necessary energy dissipation, 6 long-stroke viscous dampers were added in a UCU (Universal-Cylindrical-Universal) joint configuration, connecting the damper mass to a fixed base (or the moving building).
Print Parameters Optimized:
    • Layer Height: 0.05mm - 0.14mm (varied for detail vs. speed).
    • Infill: 2-10% Line pattern, providing internal structure while minimizing material use and print time.
    • Supports: Strategically generated only for severe overhangs (e.g., wheel arches) to facilitate clean printing and easy post-processing.

A screenshot of the model prepared in the slicer, highlighting layer lines and support structures.

3. Physical Prototyping & Assembly:
• The components were 3D printed, post-processed (support removal, light sanding), and assembled.
• Fasteners: M20 hex Allen bolts were used to attach the wheels to the axle hubs, mimicking the real-life assembly interface.
Key Skills Demonstrated:

1. Advanced CAD & Surfacing: Mastery of Fusion 360's Form/Patch workspace for creating complex, organic geometry from 2D references.
2. Blueprint Interpretation: Ability to interpret orthographic views and translate them into accurate 3D models.
3. Design for Additive Manufacturing (DFAM): Practical application of slicing parameter optimization for FDM printing.
4. 3D Printing Operation: Hands-on experience with FDM printer operation and post-processing.
